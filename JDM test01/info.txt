The selected text outlines the comprehensive logistics and administrative process of importing a used vehicle from Japan via an export agent.

## 1. Sourcing and Purchase
* **Methods:** Vehicles are primarily sourced through **Japanese car auctions** (most popular for lower prices), dealer stock, or private sales.
* **Agent Role:** Export agents (like Provide Cars) bid directly on behalf of clients and issue invoices within 24 hours.

## 2. Domestic Logistics (Japan)
* **The Yard:** A restricted area near the port where vehicles are stored and prepared. This is where cleaning, certifications, and customs documentation occur.
* **Inland Transport:** Vehicles are moved from the auction site to the yard. Costs range from **¥6,000 to ¥80,000**, depending on distance (e.g., Okinawa requires ferry transport).
* **Storage:** The first month is typically free, after which storage fees (approx. **¥500/day**) apply.

## 3. Shipping Methods
* **RORO (Roll-on-Roll-off):** The simplest method; cars are driven onto the vessel. Pricing is based on vehicle volume ($M^3$) or a flat rate.
* **Container Shipping:** Used for shipping multiple vehicles simultaneously or for non-movable cars.
* **Marine Insurance:** Optional but recommended. It is included in **CIF** (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) terms and available upon request for **FOB** (Free On Board) terms.

## 4. Country-Specific Requirements
* **Biosecurity:** Countries like Australia and New Zealand require strict quarantine declarations and heat treatments for pests.
* **Certifications:** Specific markets, such as Bangladesh, require **JAAI certification** to facilitate Letters of Credit.

## 5. Documentation and Paperwork
* **Export Certificate:** Created after deregistering the vehicle in Japan. This is the primary document needed for registration in the destination country.
* **Bill of Lading (B/L):** The title document proving ownership. It can be sent as a physical original via DHL or as a "Surrendered" electronic copy.
* **Customs Clearance:** The export agent manages Japanese customs; however, the buyer (or their local agent) must handle customs and registration upon arrival at the destination port.

---

> **Note:** While the process involves complex logistics—including vanning, drayage, and maritime law—agents typically manage the Japanese side of the transaction, leaving the buyer to focus on local compliance and final pickup.
